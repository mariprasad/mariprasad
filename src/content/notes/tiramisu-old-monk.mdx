---
title: Tiramisu — Old Monk instead of Marsala
date: "2026-06-16"
summary: There's no single "classic" booze in tiramisu — the coffee is espresso, and the traditional spirit is Marsala, not a coffee brandy. I reached for Old Monk, and it more than holds its own.
---

I went looking for the "correct" tiramisu and found there isn't really one bottle. Two separate things are doing the work:

- **The coffee** is just strong **espresso** — that's what the ladyfingers get dipped in (straight from the moka pot, in my case).
- **The spirit** is a separate splash, and the *traditional* one is **Marsala**, a fortified Italian wine. Dark rum, brandy and coffee liqueur (Kahlúa) are all common stand-ins.

I used **Old Monk**, and it more than holds its own — that vanilla-caramel warmth sits right alongside the espresso and the mascarpone. Not a compromise; if anything, I prefer it.

No baking here, technically — it's a fridge-set, no-bake assembly — but it came out of the same kitchen, and it came out *well*.
