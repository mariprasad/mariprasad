---
title: Why autolyse is better in Bangalore (most of the time)
date: "2026-06-11"
summary: In a warm kitchen, autolyse buys you control over timing, stronger dough, and safer long rests. A note on why I lean autolyse here.
---

Bangalore's kitchen warmth changes the maths on autolyse vs fermentolyse. Here's why I mostly reach for a plain autolyse.

## 1. You control fermentation timing

- No starter in the mix → no fermentation yet.
- You decide exactly when bulk begins.
- Very useful in warm weather.

## 2. Stronger dough

- Gluten develops first, without acid interference.
- Gives better structure and oven spring.

## 3. Safer for longer rests

- You can autolyse 30–60 min (even longer) without risk.
- With fermentolyse, even 30 min can "eat into" bulk time.

**Especially useful if:**

- you're doing a high-hydration dough,
- you're using whole wheat / atta,
- your kitchen crosses 27 °C.

## The problem with fermentolyse in Bangalore

Because fermentation starts immediately, you lose control over timing — and the dough can get slack, overproof faster, and turn slightly sour earlier.

Bakers in warm kitchens say exactly this:

> "At ~29 °C things ferment too fast… autolyse helps develop gluten before fermentation starts."

## Bottom line

- **Hot kitchen (Bangalore)** → autolyse wins.
- **Cool kitchen / need speed** → fermentolyse.
