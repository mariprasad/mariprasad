> **An upcoming project.** This is the 3-day method I jotted down during my sourdough classes — my next goal at the bench. Until I get there, my everyday loaf is the lighter [Semi-Sourdough](/baking/semi-sourdough); photos will land here once I've baked a few of these.

The long one. Three days from a refreshed starter to a cold-proofed, Dutch-oven loaf — but most of that time the dough is just sitting in the fridge being patient. Here's the whole process the way I run it.

## The dough

A wholegrain-forward formula. Bread flour is ideally 12.2–13.8% protein.

- Bread flour — 560 g
- Whole wheat flour — 104 g
- Rye flour — 34 g
- Wheat germ — 52 g
- Water — 562 g
- Sea salt — 16 g
- Leaven — 100 g

## The soaker (night before)

Soak the wholegrains overnight in the chiller at **1–4 °C**:

- Whole wheat flour — 104 g
- Rye flour — 34 g
- Wheat germ — 52 g
- Water — 400 g

The remaining **balance water (162 g)** and **bread flour (560 g)** go in at **18–20 °C** the next morning.

## Building a starter from scratch

If you don't have a starter going, build one over ~14 days. Start with:

- Unbleached bread flour — 180 g
- Whole wheat flour — 20 g
- Honey (not raw) — 1 g

Then feed on a schedule (equal-ish flour & water, ~20–30 g each), keeping it at room temp (23–26 °C):

- **Day 1** — 30 g water + 30 g flour + 1 g honey
- **Day 3** — 30 g starter + 30 g water + 30 g flour
- **Day 5–6** — 20 g starter + 20 g water + 20 g flour
- **Day 10** — feed twice (9 am & 9 pm)
- **Day 11–14** — 20 g starter + 20 g water + 20 g flour

Keep a **backup starter** (20 g starter + 40 g flour + 40 g water); leave it ~2 hrs at room temp, then chill — good for up to 14 days.

## Building the leaven (day before)

Feed up to a mature leaven on bake-eve:

| Time | Starter | Water | Flour | Total |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1:30 pm | 10 g | 10 g | 10 g | 30 g |
| 6:30 pm | 10 g | 10 g | 10 g | 30 g |
| 11:30 pm | 15 g | 15 g | 15 g | 45 g |
| 4:30 am | 25 g | 50 g | 50 g | 125 g |

You need **100 g** for the dough; the leftover ~25 g goes back to daily starter maintenance. Final feed at **4:30 am** → matures by **~9:30 am** → use it between **9:30–10:30 am**.

## Bake day

1. **8:00 am** — mix the autolyse (balance water + bread flour + the soaker) and let it rest.
2. **~9:45 am** — mix in the leaven, folding slowly 4–5 min. Dough temp should be **under 25 °C**, ideally **22–23 °C**. If it's at 25 °C, cling-wrap and chill 10 min, hand-fold, chill 10 min more, until it's around **16–17 °C**.
3. Rest ~30 min at 22–23 °C.
4. Mix in the **sea salt** using the pinching method, 5–6 min on slow speed.
5. **Light fold** → 30 min → **lamination** (add any toppings here) → 30 min.
6. **Coil fold ×3**, 30–45 min apart.
7. **Bulk** a further 1–2 hrs.
8. **Divide / preshape** → 10–15 min bench rest → **shape** → 10 min.
9. Into the **proofing basket** → **cold proof overnight, 12–16 hrs at 1–3 °C**.

## The bake

Straight from the fridge into a screaming-hot **Dutch oven**:

- **20 min at 250 °C — lid on**
- **15 min at 200 °C — lid off**

Cool completely before cutting. (Cutting it warm is a beginner's mistake I still make.)
