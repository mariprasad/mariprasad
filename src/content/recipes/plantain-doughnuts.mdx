Not your classic ring doughnut. These start from a rich brioche dough — nearly half its flour weight in butter — with mashed **ripe plantain** worked through, then fried and rolled in sugar while still warm. Softer, sweeter, and a little reckless.

## Brioche base dough

- Bread flour — 380 g
- T65 flour — 120 g
- Sourdough starter **or** dry yeast — 150 g **/** 7 g
- Egg — 175 g
- Milk — 100 g
- Salt — 10 g
- Sugar — 105 g
- Butter — 230 g
- …plus mashed ripe plantain, to taste

## Method

1. Mix the flours, leaven (starter or yeast), egg, milk, sugar and salt into a dough; knead to good gluten strength.
2. Add the **butter** a little at a time, kneading until smooth and glossy, then work the mashed **ripe plantain** through.
3. Bulk ferment, then **cold-proof overnight** — cold dough is far easier to shape.
4. Shape into doughnuts and proof until puffy.
5. **Fry** until deep golden, then roll in sugar while warm.
