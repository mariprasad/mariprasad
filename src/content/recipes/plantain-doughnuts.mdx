Not your classic ring doughnut. A soft, enriched yeasted dough sweetened with **roasted ripe plantain**, fried and rolled in sugar while still warm — softer and sweeter than the usual.

## Ingredients

- 2 ripe yellow plantains, roasted & mashed (~½ cup mash)
- Warm milk — 180 ml (40–41 °C / 105 °F)
- Active dry yeast — 11 g
- Sugar — 55 g
- Eggs — 2
- Unsalted butter — 113 g, melted & cooled
- All-purpose flour — 500 g (start around 450 g, add as needed)
- Salt — 8 g
- Optional: 1 tsp vanilla
- Optional: ¼ tsp nutmeg or cinnamon

## Method

1. Stir the **yeast** into the **warm milk** and let it bloom.
2. Mix in the sugar, eggs, mashed plantain, melted butter (and vanilla/spice if using).
3. Add the **flour and salt** — start with ~450 g and add more only as needed. Keep the dough soft and slightly tacky; knead until smooth.
4. **First rise** — cover and rest until doubled (~1–1.5 hrs).
5. Shape into doughnuts and **proof** until puffy (~45–60 min).
6. **Fry** until deep golden on both sides, drain, then roll in sugar while still warm.
