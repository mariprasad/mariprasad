The loaf I actually bake most weeks. **No starter here** — just a **whisper of commercial yeast (~0.5 g for a ~450 g dough)** and a long, slow proof. Given enough cold, patient time, that tiny amount of yeast still builds an open, custardy crumb and a soft tang: the flavour comes from the slow ferment, not a sourdough culture. It's my everyday bread while I work up to the full [three-day sourdough](/baking/sourdough).

## The dough

- Strong bread flour — I work to a ~450 g batch
- **Hydration — 67%, working up to 70%** as your flour and handling allow (≈ 300–315 g water per 450 g flour)
- **Yeast — ~0.5 g per 450 g** — the only leavening; the long, slow proof does the rest
- Salt — added after the mix (~2% of flour)

> Hydration is a feel thing: start at 67% and only push toward 70% once you understand your flour's strength and trust your dough handling.

## Bulk fermentation & folds

The fold rhythm I follow, adapted from [@sourdough_explained](https://www.instagram.com/sourdough_explained/)'s two approaches — pick whichever suits your day:

| Time | Approach 1 | Approach 2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 10:00 | Mix dough (water, flour, yeast) | Mix dough (water, flour, yeast) |
| 11:00 | Add salt | Add salt |
| 11:30 | Fold 1 (stretch & fold) | — |
| 12:00 | Fold 2 (coil fold) | Fold 1 (stretch & fold) |
| 12:30 | Fold 3 (coil fold) | — |
| 13:00 | Fold 4 (coil fold) | Fold 2 (coil fold) |
| 14:00 | — | Fold 3 (coil fold) |
| 15:00 | — | Fold 4 (coil fold) |
| 15:30 | Preshape (optional) | Preshape (optional) |
| 16:00 | Shape | Shape |

After shaping, cold-proof, and bake as you would a sourdough.
