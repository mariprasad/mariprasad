The one that finally tastes like *sourdough* — proper wild-culture tang — but without the three-day wait. The trick is a **store-bought dry sourdough starter** (a powdered culture) in place of yeast: it does both the lift and the flavour, and it's lively enough to bring the whole bake in within a day.

Everything else is my [Semi-Sourdough](/baking/semi-sourdough) method — same folds, same bulk, same cold proof and bake. The only real change is swapping the pinch of yeast for the dry starter, plus this flour blend.

## The dough

- All-purpose flour — 350 g
- Whole wheat flour — 50 g
- Rye flour — 35 g
- **Dry sourdough starter (powder) — 10 g** — no commercial yeast
- Salt — 8 g
- Water — 310–320 g

That's ~435 g flour at roughly **72% hydration**.

## Method

Follow the [Semi-Sourdough](/baking/semi-sourdough) process exactly — mix (water, flour, the dry starter), rest, work in the salt, then the fold rhythm, bulk, shape, cold-proof and bake. The dry starter simply slots in wherever the yeast would have gone.
