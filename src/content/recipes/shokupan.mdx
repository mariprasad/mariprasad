The one that started the whole obsession. A cooked-flour paste (tangzhong) pre-gelatinises some of the starch, which lets the dough hold more water — so the crumb stays soft and tearable for days.

## Tangzhong

Whisk together and cook over low heat until it thickens to a paste, then cool:

- Flour — 30 g
- Milk — 220 g

## Dough

- Strong bread flour — 370 g
- Milk powder — 20 g
- Sugar — 40 g
- Instant yeast — 8 g
- Salt — 10 g
- Butter — 60 g
- Milk — 60 g
- 1 egg + 1 yolk
- …plus the cooled tangzhong

## Method

1. Mix everything except the butter into a shaggy dough, then knead until it starts to come together.
2. Add the **butter** and knead to a smooth, strong, windowpane dough.
3. Bulk ferment until roughly doubled.
4. Divide, shape into rolls, and nestle them into a loaf tin (or bake free-form as a pull-apart).
5. Proof until puffy and risen near the rim.
6. Bake at **~180 °C** until deep golden and hollow-sounding, ~25–30 min. Brush with butter while warm for that glossy top.
