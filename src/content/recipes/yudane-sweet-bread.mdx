A soft, sweet milk bread built on a **yudane** — a cooked-flour paste, tangzhong's close cousin — plus a little curd for tenderness. Built on a 250 g total-flour recipe. The mixing order really matters here, so follow it in sequence.

## Yudane paste

- Flour — 12 g (from the 250 g total)
- Water — 60 g (just-boiled)

Mix to a paste and let it rest and cool (a few hours, or overnight, is ideal).

## Dough

- Flour — 200 g (the remaining flour)
- All of the yudane paste
- Milk powder — 10 g
- Sugar — 30 g
- Salt — 5 g
- Yeast — 3–4 g
- Butter — 25 g, soft
- Curd — 15 ml
- Water — 100–110 ml

## Method (the order matters)

1. **Mix first:** flour, milk powder, sugar, yeast, water, curd, and the yudane. Mix until a rough dough forms, then knead **5 minutes**.
2. **Then add** the salt and soft butter. Knead another **6–8 minutes**, until smooth and stretchy.
3. The dough should feel **soft and slightly tacky — not stiff**.
