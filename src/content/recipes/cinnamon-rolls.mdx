A soft, enriched dough that bakes up pale-golden and pillowy. Keep it slightly tacky and shape it tight, and you get that bakery look.

## Dough

- All-purpose flour — 440 g
- Sugar — 60 g (up to 70 g for sweeter buns)
- Salt — 6 g
- Yeast — 7 g (1 packet)
- Egg — 1
- Milk — 240 ml (start with ~220 ml, adjust as needed)
- Butter — 50 g, softened

## Method

1. **Mix** — warm milk (not hot) + yeast; add the sugar and egg; add flour + salt and mix to a dough.
2. **Knead** 5–8 min, then add the **butter** slowly and knead until smooth. Aim for a light windowpane (doesn't need to be perfect).
3. **First rise** — cover and rest 1–1.5 hrs, until doubled.
4. **Shape** — divide into equal balls (~60–80 g each); shape tight and round for smooth buns.
5. **Second rise** — 45–60 min, until puffy.
6. **Bake (convection)** — 170–175 °C for 15–18 min. Don't overbake — they should stay pale golden.

## Finishing

Right after baking, brush with **milk or butter**. For extra shine and softness, a light **sugar syrup** (water + sugar) works beautifully.

## Notes

- Keep the dough soft and slightly tacky — don't over-flour.
- Tight, smooth shaping is what gives the bakery look.
