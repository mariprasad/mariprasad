A proper enriched brioche — nearly half its flour weight in butter. This batch I turned into **ripe-plantain doughnuts**: mashed ripe plantain worked through the dough, then fried and rolled in sugar — softer and sweeter than a classic ring doughnut. The same dough also bakes beautifully as a loaf or buns.

## Dough

- Bread flour — 380 g
- T65 flour — 120 g
- Sourdough starter **or** dry yeast — 150 g **/** 7 g
- Egg — 175 g
- Milk — 100 g
- Salt — 10 g
- Sugar — 105 g
- Butter — 230 g

## Method

1. Mix the flours, leaven (starter or yeast), egg, milk, sugar and salt into a dough; knead until it develops good gluten strength.
2. Add the **butter** a little at a time, kneading until each addition is fully incorporated and the dough is smooth and glossy.
3. Bulk ferment, then **cold-proof overnight** in the fridge — cold dough is far easier to shape.
4. Shape (loaf, buns, or doughnuts), proof until puffy.
5. **Bake** at ~180 °C until deep golden — or, for the plantain doughnuts, work mashed ripe plantain through the dough, **fry**, and roll in sugar while warm.
