A soft, rich sweet bread built on the same enriched dough I make for cinnamon rolls. Shaped into tight rounds and baked together, it pulls apart into a pillowy, golden loaf. My best sweet bread so far.

## Dough

- All-purpose flour — 440 g
- Sugar — 60 g (up to 70 g for a sweeter loaf)
- Salt — 6 g
- Yeast — 7 g (1 packet)
- Egg — 1
- Milk — 240 ml (start with ~220 ml, adjust as needed)
- Butter — 50 g, softened

## Method

1. **Mix** — warm milk (not hot) + yeast; add the sugar and egg; add flour + salt and mix to a dough.
2. **Knead** 5–8 min, then add the **butter** slowly and knead until smooth. Aim for a light windowpane.
3. **First rise** — cover and rest 1–1.5 hrs, until doubled.
4. **Shape** — divide into ~60–80 g balls, shape tight and round, and nestle them together in a tin.
5. **Second rise** — 45–60 min, until puffy.
6. **Bake** — 170–175 °C for 15–18 min. Brush with milk or butter straight from the oven for shine; a light sugar syrup keeps it soft.

## Notes

- Keep the dough soft and slightly tacky — don't over-flour.
- Tight, smooth shaping is what gives that bakery pull-apart look.
- **Dial it rustic:** the same dough with less sugar and butter bakes into a deeper-crusted, more rustic loaf (the darker one in the photos) — less rich, just as soft.
